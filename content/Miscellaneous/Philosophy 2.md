## Wittgenstein's Philosophy
The worlds is everything that is the case; i.e., the world is the totality of facts (and divides into facts).
	This view on the world aligns w/ the correspondence theory of truth...

**Sense:** The conditions under which a sentence/proposition can be verified or falsified
	From this, it can be seen that tautologies (e.g., $p\vee\neg p$ in classical logic) and contradictions 
	($p\wedge\neg p$) are 'senseless' as there are no exist no condition where they can be verified or falsified (as they are always true or always false). However, it is maintained that such propositions are not nonsense.

**Pictures:** Language is a framework to express ideas and characteristics about the world. Hence, a proposition may be imagined as a 'picture' due to being an expression of a particular characteristic. According to Wittgenstein, a proposition and the reality it describes must share the same logical form.
