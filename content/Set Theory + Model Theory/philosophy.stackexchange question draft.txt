Title: Does the gap-theorist approach towards the strengthened liar suffice?

I have been researching many-valued logics (paracomplete and paraconsistent) and hierarchies of languages

Let T (⌈ϕ⌉) means ”ϕ is true”
F (⌈ϕ⌉) = T (⌈¬ϕ⌉) means ”ϕ is false”
U (⌈ϕ⌉) means ”ϕ is undefined”
¬T (⌈ϕ⌉) = F (⌈ϕ⌉) or U (⌈ϕ⌉) (i.e., untrue)
¬F (⌈ϕ⌉) = T (⌈ϕ⌉) or U (⌈ϕ⌉) (i.e., unfalse)
¬U (⌈ϕ⌉) = T (⌈ϕ⌉) or F (⌈ϕ⌉) (i.e., defined)
The Strengthened Liar/Revenge of The Liar:
Consider the statement Q = ”This sentence is false or undefined”
Q ≡ F (⌈Q⌉) ∨ U (⌈Q⌉)
T (⌈Q⌉) = T (⌈F (⌈Q⌉) ∨ U (⌈Q⌉)⌉)
Q is true is if it is false or undefined
